THE CORE OF THE SPORT:

COMPETITION.

Streetlifting is a judged strength sport where athletes compete in weighted pull-ups, weighted dips, the barbell squat and weighted muscle-ups while being organized by bodyweight categories.

Three attempts are allowed per lift, the goal is to achieve the highest successful lift within each category.

The sport emerged from the street workout scene but evolved into something more structured: real federations, real weight classes, real records. If powerlifting and calisthenics merged, this would be the result.

HOW IT WORKS

COMPETITION FORMAT

01

WEIGH IN

Athletes compete within their bodyweight category. Classes typically run from 60kg up to 100kg+. Your total is compared against others in your class.

02

THREE ATTEMPTS

Each athlete gets three attempts per lift. Here strategy matters: your opener should be conservative, your third attempt is your max. One successful lift is enough to register a total.

03

JUDGED REPS

Every rep is judged for range of motion. A pull-up requires chin above bar from dead hang. A dip requires full lockout to 90° at the bottom.

04

YOUR TOTAL

Your best successful attempt on each lift adds up to your competition total. Highest total in the weight class wins.
