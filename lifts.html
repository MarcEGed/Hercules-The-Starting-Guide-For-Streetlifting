PULL

WEIGHTED PULL-UP

The king of upper body pulling. In competition, extra weight is added via a belt, vest, or between the legs. Every rep is judged from dead hang to chin over bar.

COMPETITION STANDARD

  • Start from a dead hang, arms fully extended, no kipping
  • Chin must clearly pass the bar on every rep
  • Controlled descent back to full extension
  • No leg drive, no kipping, no swinging
  • Weight must be secured and not touch the bar

COMMON RED LIGHTS

  • Chin not clearing the bar (most common)
  • Not returning to full dead hang between reps
  • Excessive kipping or body swing
  • Weight touching the bar or structure

HOW TO TRAIN IT

Build your base to 15+ clean bodyweight reps before adding load. Then add weight slowly, in 2.5kg increments. Train twice per week: one heavy day (3-5 reps weighted), one volume day (higher reps at lighter load). Accessory work: lat pulldowns, straight arm pulldowns, dead hangs for grip.

PUSH

WEIGHTED DIP

The pressing counterpart to the pull-up. Performed on parallel bars with added weight. Requires full lockout at the top and 90° elbow bend at the bottom minimum.

COMPETITION STANDARD

  • Start at full lockout, arms completely straight
  • Lower until upper arm is parallel to the ground (90° at elbow)
  • Return to full lockout, no partial reps
  • Body stays controlled, no excessive forward lean
  • Feet must not touch the bars or ground

COMMON RED LIGHTS

  • Not reaching 90° depth at the bottom
  • Not locking out fully at the top
  • Feet touching the structure
  • Excessive forward lean beyond what's needed

HOW TO TRAIN IT

Reach 20+ clean bodyweight dips before loading. Add weight in 5kg steps. Heavy day: 3-5 reps at 85-90% max. Volume day: multiple sets of 8-12 at moderate weight. Accessory: tricep pushdowns, chest press, shoulder stability work.

LEGS

BARBELL SQUAT

The raw strength anchor of streetlifting. A back squat judged to competition depth, hip crease below knee. Often the lift that separates the competitors from the contenders.

COMPETITION STANDARD

  • Bar on upper back (high or low bar, athlete's choice)
  • Hip crease must pass below the top of the knee at the bottom
  • Stand to full lockout at the top
  • No squat suit or wraps unless federation allows
  • Wait for the judge's signal before beginning and racking

COMMON RED LIGHTS

  • Depth not reached (most common fault in competition)
  • Not waiting for the rack signal
  • Forward lean causing bar to shift
  • Knee cave at the bottom

HOW TO TRAIN IT

Train depth from day one; never squat to a box that's too high. Program linear progression: add 2.5–5kg per session when you're newer. Run 3–5 rep sets for competition prep. Accessory: Romanian deadlifts, leg press, Bulgarian split squats, core work.

SKILL

WEIGHTED MUSCLE-UP

The technical crown of the sport. A bar muscle-up performed with additional weight, requiring both pulling and pushing strength plus the transition. The hardest lift to judge cleanly.

COMPETITION STANDARD

  • Start from dead hang
  • Pull through the bar to a full dip lockout at the top
  • No kipping (strict muscle-up only, federation dependent)
  • Arms fully extended at the top before descent
  • Controlled return to dead hang

COMMON RED LIGHTS

  • Not locking out at the top
  • Excessive kipping (federation dependent)
  • Not returning to dead hang
  • Incomplete transition (chest not clearing the bar)

HOW TO TRAIN IT

You need a strict muscle-up before adding any weight. Period. Build to 5+ clean bodyweight reps first. Then add 2.5kg at a time; transitions get harder fast with load. Train the pull and the dip separately at higher loads than your muscle-up max. Technique is everything here.
